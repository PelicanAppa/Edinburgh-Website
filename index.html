Why you should visit Edinburgh during Christmastime!



What is Edinburgh?

Edingburgh is the capital city of Scotland and is filled with lots of tourists
and food every year (especially in the winter during Christmastime), like Haggis
(a Scottish dish consisting of a sheep's or calf's offal mixed with suet, oatmeal,
and seasoning and boiled in a bag, traditionally one made from the animal's
stomach), mince and tatties, Scotch pies, etc.

It might not snow much here in the winter but it does get cold so you might
want to get warm and fuzzy clothes so that you can stay unfrozen.

There are loads of fun rides like the Twister and others. But the images for
those are too fast to see so here are some less graphic pictures instead.


The Christmasmarket is always open during the day till the night and has a
few rides along with the big Ferris Wheel that you get every year. The
queues might be a bit long taking up to 20 minutes but you get a
magnificent view of Edinburgh's city centre.

And from Santa Land, the more fun one in my opinion, you can get good
photo sites to take pictures of the castle.
